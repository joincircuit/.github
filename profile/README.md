# Circuit

the uk’s network for teens who code.

Circuit is a community and platform for young builders in the UK. if you’re the person in your school who fixes the wi‑fi, ships side projects, or stays up too late tweaking code, this is for you.

we run events, help you meet other builders, and give you space to ship the weird, brilliant ideas in your head — no experience needed, just curiosity.
[become a circuit member here →](https://circuit.okunde.org)

---

## what Circuit is

- **for teens, built by teens:** made in the UK, focused on people still in school or college.
- **community first:** Discord, events, and projects where you actually talk to other humans.
- **low‑pressure, high‑support:** no cringe hustle culture, just a calm place to learn and build.

---

## what you can do with Circuit

- **join the community:** meet other young builders, share what you’re working on, ask for help.
- **attend events:** hack nights, online meetups, and IRL sessions around the UK.
- **ship projects:** get feedback, find collaborators, and maybe even sponsors.

---

## who Circuit is for

Circuit is for you if:

- you like making things with code (or want to learn)
- you’d rather share a repo link than a selfie
- you want a space that feels more like a cosy Discord server than a corporate programme
- you’re based in the UK (or connected to it somehow)

whether you’ve shipped ten projects or just opened your first tutorial, you’re welcome.

## contributing

Circuit is built by its community. if you’d like to help:

1. fork the repo  
2. create a feature or fix branch  
3. make your changes (keep to the Communication Handguide where it touches copy)  
4. open a pull request with a short, clear description of what you’ve changed  

be kind in reviews, assume good intent, and keep feedback specific and constructive.

if you’re unsure where to start, open a small issue or draft PR and say so — we’re happy to point you in the right direction.
